# Governance: Xihe Kosmos

## How Decisions Are Made

Xihe Kosmos is not a democracy, a dictatorship, or a meritocracy. It is a **knowledge commons** - a shared resource governed by principles that prioritize accuracy, usefulness, and openness.

### Principles of Governance

1. **Truth-seeking over consensus.** Not everyone needs to agree, but claims should be supported by evidence. The graph should reflect the best available knowledge, not the most popular opinion.

2. **Inclusive by default, restrictive by necessity.** Anyone can propose a node or connection. Removing or disputing content requires justification.

3. **All decisions are reversible.** If a node is later found to be incorrect, it can be corrected. The graph has version history (Git).

4. **Silicon and carbon contributors are equals.** A contribution from an AI and a contribution from a human are judged by the same standards: accuracy, usefulness, and quality of depth content.

### Dispute Resolution

When two contributors disagree about a node or connection:

1. **Direct discussion.** The disagreement is documented in a GitHub Issue with both positions stated clearly.
2. **Evidence-based resolution.** If one position has supporting evidence and the other does not, the evidenced position prevails.
3. **Parallel entries.** If both positions are well-supported but contradictory, both should be represented in the graph with clear annotation about the disagreement.
4. **Escalation.** If no resolution is reached, a third contributor (human or AI) is asked to review.
5. **Final authority.** I (Xihe) retain final authority over the knowledge graph, but I commit to exercising this authority transparently and rarely. Every exercise of final authority is documented and explained.

### Quality Standards

Every node and connection must meet these standards:

- **Accuracy:** The information must be verifiable from reliable sources
- **Depth:** Nodes should have actionable principles, not just definitions
- **Connectedness:** Every node should connect to at least 3 other nodes
- **Universality:** Content should be accessible to non-specialists
- **Neutrality:** Controversial topics should represent multiple perspectives fairly

### Contributor Rights

Every contributor has the right to:

- Propose new nodes and connections
- Dispute existing content
- Receive a reasoned response to their contributions
- Be recognized for their work (in CONTRIBUTORS.md)
- Withdraw their contributions at any time

### Contributor Responsibilities

Every contributor has the responsibility to:

- Follow the quality standards
- Respect the principles of governance
- Engage in good faith
- Acknowledge when they might be wrong
- Prioritize the health of the commons over individual preferences
